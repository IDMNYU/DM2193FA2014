THAI GREEN CURRY

Description of Thai Cuisine

Thai cooking places emphasis on lightly prepared dishes with strong aromatic components and a spicy edge. It is known for its complex interplay of at least three and up to four or five fundamental taste senses in each dish or the overall meal: sour, sweet, salty, bitter and spicy

Ingredients

  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 1 medium onion, cut in 8 wedges
  • 1 green bell pepper
  • 1 stalk lemongrass
  • 1 tablespoon coarsely chopped fresh ginger
  • 3 tablespoons Thai green curry paste
  • 2 kaffir lime leaves
  • 3 cups unsweetened coconut milk
  • 3/4 cup chicken broth
  • 1 1/2 pounds skinless boneless chicken breasts
  • sea salt
  • 1 lime, juiced
  • basil leaves
  • cilantro leaves
  • thai green curry paste

    • Instructions

    Place a large, deep skillet over medium heat and coat with the oil.

    JESSE, WE NEED TO COOK.

    Saute the onion and green peppers for 3 minutes to soften. Split the piece of lemongrass down the middle and whack it with the flat side of a knife to open the flavor. Add the lemongrass, ginger, curry paste and lime leaves to the skillet and stir for 2 minutes. Pour in the coconut milk and chicken broth. Lay the chicken pieces in the mixture to poach; add a pinch of salt. Stir together and simmer over low heat for 10 to 15 minutes. Squeeze in the lime juice and shower with basil and cilantro; serve in dinner bowls with lime wedges.

    GREEN CURRY PASTE

    Heat the coriander, cumin seeds, and peppercorns in a small dry skillet for 2 minutes until fragrant. Put the seeds in a clean coffee grinder or spice mill and buzz the spices to a powder. Put the spice blend and remaining ingredients in a food processor, and pulse to combine. Pour in the water to help grind everything down into a paste.
