Zucchini Pancake

Ingredients

  • 3 large zucchini
  • 1 extra-large egg white
  • 2 tablespoons almond meal
  • 1 cup (2 sticks) butter, softened
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • Salt and pepper
  • 3 tablespoons clarified butter, ghee, or unsalted butter

Instructions

  1. Using either a hand-held grater or a food processor fitted with the shredding blade, shred the zucchini.
  2. Place the shredded zucchini in the center of a large, clean kitchen towel. Bring the sides up and twist hard to express all of the liquid. You may have to do this a few times to get all the liquid out; the drier the zucchini, the crisper the pancakes will be.
  3. Place the shredded, drained zucchini in a large mixing bowl. Add the egg white, almond meal, paprika, and salt and pepper to taste, tossing to blend in the seasonings.
  4. Heat the clarified butter in a large frying pan over medium heat. When very hot but not smoking, spoon in the zucchini mixture to make individual circles about 3-1/2 inches in diameter (you will need to do this in batches). Fry, turning once, until cooked through, golden brown, and crisp, about 5 minutes.
  5. Using a spatula, transfer to a double layer of paper towels to drain. If necessary, place on a baking sheet in a low oven to keep warm until ready to serve.

Buttermilk Biscuit

Ingredients

  • 2 1/2 cups of blanched almond flour
  • 1/4 cup + 1 tbsp of organic arrowroot powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon of baking soda
  • 1/2 teaspoon of sea salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon of baking powder
  • 3 tbsp healthy solid fat (butter, tallow, lard, ghee, schmaltz or coconut oil)
  • 1 tbsp honey or maple syrup
  • 1/4 cup cultured buttermilk or raw cultured sour cream
  • 2 large eggs
  • 2 tbsp butter, melted

Instructions

  1. Prehead oven to 375° F.

  2. Preheat your oven to 350 Degrees F

  3. Line a cookie sheet with unbleached parchment paper

  4. In a medium bowl combine Almond Flour, Baking Soda, Baking Powder & Sea salt

  5. In a small pot, over low heat combine your fat, honey and buttermilk or sour cream, whisk well until melted, remove from heat

  6. Add 2 eggs to your liquids and whisk well to combine

  7. Pour liquid/egg mixture to the dry ingredients and stir well

  8. Fold in ¼c Arrowroot powder

  9. Divide dough into 8 equal portions, using 1T of arrowroot powder to dust your hands. Form each section in to a biscuit shape and place on parchment lined cookie sheet

  10. Bake your biscuits at 350 for 18-20 minutes on the middle oven rack - brushing with melted butter ½ way through baking

  11. Allow your biscuits to cool for a minimum of 15 minutes before enjoying!

Chocolate Cake Recipe

Ingredients

  • Cooking spray"
  • 1 cup unsweetened cocoa powder (natural or Dutch process)
  • 2 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 2 cups sugar
  • 1 1/2 teaspoons baking powder
  • 1 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 3 large eggs, at room temperature
  • 3/4 cup vegetable oil
  • 1/2 cup sour cream
  • 2 teaspoons vanilla extract

Instructions

  1. Preheat the oven to 350 degrees F. Coat two 9-inch-round cake pans with cooking spray and line the bottoms with parchment paper.

  2. Whisk the cocoa powder and 1 1/2 cups boiling water in a medium bowl until smooth; set aside.

  3. Whisk the flour, sugar, baking powder, baking soda and salt in a large bowl until combined.

  4. Add the eggs, vegetable oil, sour cream and vanilla and beat with a mixer on medium speed until smooth, about 1 minute.

  5. Reduce the mixer speed to low; beat in the cocoa mixture in a steady stream until just combined, then finish mixing with a rubber spatula. (The batter will be thin.)

  6. Divide the batter between the prepared pans and tap the pans against the counter to help the batter settle.

  7. Bake until a toothpick inserted into the middle comes out clean, 30 to 40 minutes.

  8. Transfer to racks and let cool 10 minutes, then run a knife around the edge of the pans and turn the cakes out onto the racks to cool completely.

  9. Remove the parchment. Trim the tops of the cakes with a long serrated knife to make them level, if desired.
